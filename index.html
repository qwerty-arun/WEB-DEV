Priority Inversion

Definition Priority inversion occurs when a high-priority process is forced to wait for a low-priority process to release a resource, but that low-priority process is delayed because medium-priority processes keep running.This “inverts” the expected priority order — a low-priority task indirectly blocks a high-priority one.

Why Semantics Matter

Semantic HTML makes your code easier to read and improves accessibility for screen readers.

SEO Benefits

Search engines love well-structured pages because they understand your content better.
